Day 8 – German A1: Questions & Answers
1️⃣ W-Questions (Fragen mit „W“)

These are used to ask information in German.

German	English	Example
Wer	Who	Wer bist du? → Who are you?
Was	What	Was machst du? → What are you doing?
Wo	Where	Wo wohnst du? → Where do you live?
Wie	How	Wie heißt du? → What is your name? / How are you?
Wann	When	Wann kommst du? → When are you coming?
Warum	Why	Warum lernst du Deutsch? → Why are you learning German?
Welcher / Welche / Welches	Which	Welches Buch liest du? → Which book are you reading?

Tip: W-questions are always followed by the verb in the 2nd position:
Wie heißt du? → verb = heißt is 2nd

2️⃣ Speaking Q&A Practice

Focus on introducing yourself and answering simple questions.

A. Questions about Name

Wie heißt du? → What’s your name?

Short answer: Ich heiße [Name]. → I am called [Name]

B. Questions about Job

Was machst du? → What is your job?

Short answer: Ich bin Lehrer / Student / Arzt. → I am a teacher/student/doctor

C. Questions about City / Location

Wo wohnst du? → Where do you live?

Short answer: Ich wohne in Addis Abeba. → I live in Addis Ababa

D. Questions about Age (optional)

Wie alt bist du? → How old are you?

Short answer: Ich bin 22 Jahre alt. → I am 22 years old

3️⃣ Writing Short Answers
Practice writing short answers for these questions:
Question	Answer
Wer bist du?	Ich bin Surafel.
Wie heißt du?	Ich heiße Surafel Mengist.
Was machst du?	Ich bin Student.
Wo wohnst du?	Ich wohne in Addis Abeba.
Wie alt bist du?	Ich bin 22 Jahre alt.

Tip: Try to write 5 new sentences every day, then say them aloud.

4️⃣ Mini Speaking Drill (5–10 min)

Ask yourself 5 W-questions randomly.

Answer aloud in short sentences.

Example:

Q: Wer bist du?

A: Ich bin Surafel.

Q: Wo wohnst du?

A: Ich wohne in Addis Abeba.

Q: Was machst du?

A: Ich bin Student.

5️⃣ Listening Practice

Watch Easy German beginner videos on introductions, personal info, and basic questions.

Repeat the phrases and try to answer like in the exam.

6️⃣ Homework / Exam Prep

Write 10 W-questions and answer them in short sentences.

Record yourself speaking for 5 minutes introducing yourself.

Practice shadowing Easy German videos about daily life.

💡 Pro Tip:
For the A1 exam, questions about name, city, job, hobbies are extremely common. If you memorize these short answers, you’ll be fully prepared for speaking and writing sections.
