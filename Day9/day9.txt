Day 9 – Negation (nicht / kein → English: NOT / NO)

In English we usually use:

1️⃣ NOT – to make a sentence negative

Example:

I am not hungry.

She is not drinking coffee.

We do not eat meat.

Structure:

Subject + auxiliary verb + NOT + verb

Examples:

I do not like tea.

He does not eat pizza.

They are not drinking milk.

Short forms (very common):

do not → don’t

does not → doesn’t

is not → isn’t

are not → aren’t

Examples:

I don’t drink coffee.

She doesn’t like fish.

2️⃣ NO / NONE

Used for nouns.

Examples:

I have no money.

There is no water.

We have no bread.

🗣️ Speaking – Ordering Food

Example conversation in a restaurant.

Dialogue 1

Waiter: Hello, what would you like?
You: I would like chicken and rice.
Waiter: What would you like to drink?
You: I would like water.

Dialogue 2

Waiter: What would you like?
You: I would like pizza.
Waiter: Anything to drink?
You: Yes, orange juice please.

Dialogue 3 (Negation)

Waiter: Do you want coffee?
You: No, I don’t want coffee. I want tea.
